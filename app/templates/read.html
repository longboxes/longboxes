Fit-height: the ```` BOX is ``h-full w-full`` (so a short page still scales up to fill the stage) and ``object-contain`` handles the actual bitmap — it scales the page within those bounds preserving the aspect ratio. This matters for landscape pages on a phone: under the older ``h-full w-auto`` rule the Tailwind preflight's implicit ``max-width: 100%`` would cap the width while ``height: 100%`` was still forcing the full height, smushing the page. With ``object-contain`` the bitmap fits to whichever dimension reaches the bound first, so landscape pages letterbox cleanly and portrait pages still fill the height.
